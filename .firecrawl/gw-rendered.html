our process

What happens when I’m charged?

01 Initial Interview

This takes place either on the side of the road or at the police station when the police first contact you about an alleged offence and your involvement in it.
You should always seek legal advice before speaking to any member of the Victorian police when concerned with an allegation.

02 You Are Charged

Once police have completed their interview with you, if they feel they have sufficient evidence to have the allegations found proven, they will charge you.
They will issue you with paperwork outlining the offences you are charged with. This paperwork is vital to our defence.

03 Summon Or Bail?

Whether you are summoned to court or bailed to appear at court depends on the charges.
Bail is when the police have released you into the custody of a person who gives an undertaking that you will appear at a specified time and place to answer the charge against you. Have you been bailed to appear at court?

04 Court Appearance

The final step is attending court for your case to be heard by a magistrate or judge. Any case that goes before a court is serious. Court opens at 9am each day and we will be there to ensure you do not face this stressful task alone.
We speak to the prosecutors, court staff and manage the whole process by standing by you and speaking on your behalf for your best interests.

Frequently asked question

Do you still have questions?

01Does Garde Wilson Lawyers handle both traffic offences and serious criminal charges in Victoria?

Yes. Garde Wilson Lawyers represent clients across the full spectrum of criminal matters — from traffic and driving offences, including drink and drug driving, to serious indictable crimes such as assault, drug trafficking, sexual offences, and homicide. No matter the charge, our team provides strong, experienced defence and clear guidance at every stage of the process.

02What questions should I ask when contacting a criminal defence lawyer in Melbourne?

If you’re calling a firm like Garde Wilson Lawyers in Melbourne about a criminal defence matter, it’s important to ask the right questions to understand your options and feel confident in your representation. We recommend asking:

Have you handled cases like mine in Victorian courts before?

What’s your approach to defending this type of charge?

Based on your experience, what are the possible outcomes in my situation?

How are your legal fees structured — do you offer fixed or capped fees?

What are the next steps if I decide to proceed with your firm?

Is there anything urgent I should do before my first court date?

Who will be my main point of contact throughout the process?

How often will I receive updates about my case?

Do you provide after-hours or emergency legal support?

What should I bring or prepare before our first consultation?

These questions will help you make an informed decision and ensure you’re working with a lawyer who’s experienced, transparent, and committed to protecting your rights.

03What steps should I take right away if I’m arrested or charged with a criminal offence in Melbourne?
If you’re arrested or charged in Melbourne, contact a criminal defence lawyer immediately. Avoid answering police questions or signing any statements until you’ve received legal advice. Anything you say or do can be used against you later, so having an experienced lawyer by your side from the start is the best way to protect your rights and guide your next steps.
04What are the chances of avoiding a criminal conviction for a first-time offence?
If it’s your first offence, you may be eligible for a diversion program or another sentencing option that avoids a criminal conviction. The outcome depends on the type of offence, the circumstances of your case, and your personal history. An experienced criminal lawyer can assess your situation, negotiate on your behalf, and help you explore every opportunity to keep your record clean.
05I live in regional Victoria — can Garde Wilson Lawyers still represent me?
Yes. Garde Wilson Lawyers represent clients across all parts of Victoria, not just Melbourne. Our team regularly appears in regional courts and can provide legal advice, support, and representation remotely or in person. No matter where you’re based, you’ll have direct access to experienced criminal defence lawyers who will guide you through every stage of your case.
06Will I know the total cost of representation upfront?
Yes. We offer affordable fixed fees, giving you full clarity and confidence about costs from the beginning.
07How do legislative changes in Victoria, including updates to bail or hoon laws, impact my case?
Changes to Victorian law — such as updates to bail, hoon, or sentencing laws — can directly influence how your case is managed and the penalties that may apply. An experienced criminal defence lawyer will review your situation under the most current legislation to make sure you understand your rights, the possible outcomes, and the best strategy for your defence.
08Can digital evidence, such as mobile phone data or social media posts, be used in a criminal case against me?

Yes. Police and prosecutors can use digital evidence — including mobile phone data, text messages, photos, videos, and social media activity — to support their case. It’s important to avoid posting or sharing anything related to your matter and to seek legal advice immediately. Your lawyer can guide you on how to protect your privacy and ensure your rights are protected.
